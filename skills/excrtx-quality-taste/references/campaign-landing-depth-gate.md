# Campaign Landing Depth Gate

Use this reference when a landing page belongs to an established campaign, institution, educational offer, event, or public-service journey. It prevents a visually polished but strategically empty page.

## Failure this gate prevents

A page fails even when responsive and technically valid if it:

- reduces a rich offer to names, dates, and generic cards;
- ignores canonical descriptions, official links, approved assets, or campaign concepts;
- treats the design system as a palette rather than a meaning system;
- uses a concept only as a headline instead of shaping navigation and hierarchy;
- sends every item to the same generic destination when specific official destinations exist;
- leaves relevant conversion or evidence channels disconnected from the journey;
- could be reused for another organization by replacing the logo and colors.

## Cooperation model

For content-rich campaign pages, combine these roles before implementation:

### Creative direction

Owns the perceptual idea, composition, rhythm, visual hierarchy, interaction model, and rejection of generic solutions.

### Editorial direction

Owns the page thesis, information architecture, canonical facts, descriptions, labels, summaries, source traceability, and review status.

### Product/service journey

Owns audience decisions, next steps, conversion paths, channel integration, and friction reduction.

### Engineering

Owns semantic implementation, responsive states, accessibility, performance, progressive enhancement, and verification.

One agent may perform all roles, but it must execute them as distinct passes. Do not let the HTML pass silently substitute for creative or editorial work.

## Context inventory before design

Read or locate:

1. governance and publication restrictions;
2. campaign brief, objectives, audiences, and priority offers;
3. canonical data for every offer/item;
4. approved language, local themes, slogans, placeholders, and prohibited claims;
5. official visual assets and brand application rules;
6. verified destinations: item sites, catalogs, application portal, contact channels, social evidence, and support services;
7. status of each fact: approved, review, placeholder, blocked, or deprecated.

If the repository has a canonical structured source, use it to build the content inventory. Do not consume fields marked blocked. Review-state content may appear only in an explicit DRAFT with its gate visible.

## Offer-depth matrix

For each course, product, service, plan, event track, or opportunity, inventory:

| Field | Required behavior |
|---|---|
| Name | Exact canonical display name |
| Audience | Who the offer serves or prerequisite state |
| Short description | Concrete mechanism/content, no generic benefit claim |
| Key facts | Duration, mode, schedule, location, price/status when verified |
| Content/focus | What the person will study, receive, compare, or do |
| Fields of action | Informative destinations or applications; no promise of outcome |
| Primary CTA | Best verified specific destination |
| Secondary CTA | Catalog, source, FAQ, or general portal |
| Evidence channel | Relevant social, institutional, support, or contact surface |
| Review state | Approved, review, placeholder, or blocked |

A landing with multiple offers should not ship until this matrix is populated or missing fields are visibly governed.

## Concept-to-interface test

Write the campaign concept in one sentence. Then answer:

- What does it group?
- What does it order?
- What does it let the visitor choose?
- What changes on screen because this concept exists?

If removing the concept changes only the headline, the concept is decorative. Rebuild the information architecture.

Examples of structural use:

- a theme becomes the primary navigation taxonomy;
- a progression becomes a connected journey rather than isolated cards;
- a set of territories becomes tabs, chapters, or filters;
- an approved question becomes the decision prompt that routes the visitor;
- a campaign asset becomes a compositional anchor, not a logo pasted into a template.

## Channel integration

Integrate channels by function:

- application portal: conversion and regulatory truth;
- item/course sites: depth and specificity;
- catalog/source: factual verification;
- WhatsApp/contact: orientation and objection handling;
- Instagram/social: current evidence, spaces, people, and campaign continuity;
- support service: permanence, accessibility, or risk reduction.

Use only verified channels. A direct link with useful prefilled context is better than a fake embedded feed. Label general contacts as general; do not imply campaign exclusivity.

## Composition gate

Before implementation, choose one compositional thesis and state it explicitly. Reject these defaults unless the content truly calls for them:

- centered hero followed by three equal feature cards;
- repeated left-text/right-image sections;
- nine equal cards with only name and one metadata line;
- decorative bento with no information hierarchy;
- a stock-photo hero disconnected from the offer structure.

Prefer structures derived from the domain: journeys, levels, territories, comparisons, timelines, catalogs, maps, or decision paths.

## Critique sequence

Review in this order:

1. **Thesis:** What decision or understanding does the page enable?
2. **Center of gravity:** What dominates perception and why?
3. **Specificity:** What proves this page belongs to this campaign?
4. **Substance:** Can the visitor understand and compare the offers?
5. **Journey:** Are the next steps and channels connected?
6. **Governance:** Are review states and blocked facts handled honestly?
7. **Execution:** Responsive layout, interactions, focus, contrast, console, links, and performance.

Do not polish typography while the thesis, content depth, or journey is weak.

## Rejection tests

Reject and recompose when any answer is “yes”:

- Could a logo/color swap turn this into another institution's page?
- Did the design ignore existing descriptions or official destinations?
- Is the campaign concept absent from navigation or interaction?
- Are offers impossible to compare beyond name and schedule?
- Are important channels buried in the footer?
- Did visual simplification erase meaningful complexity?
- Did the page invent claims to compensate for missing substance?

## Verification evidence

Before delivery, record:

- canonical sources read;
- number of offers represented and depth fields covered;
- specific official destinations included;
- channels integrated and their verified role;
- desktop and true mobile viewport dimensions;
- heading line count, touch target size, document overflow, menu state, tab/filter state, expandable content state, and console errors;
- remaining approval or factual gates;
- whether the previous version was preserved when the rework changed the concept substantially.
